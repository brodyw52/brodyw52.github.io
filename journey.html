14

I started building at 14. During Covid, I sold instrumental music online and learned demand, pricing, and distribution. I also ran a small eBay operation and took a margin. In parallel, I trained seriously in piano and built discipline under pressure.

college

I entered college as a varsity soccer player. A torn ACL abruptly ended that path and created downtime. I used it to go all-in on building—learning tools, experimenting, and moving from idea to working system quickly.

build

I shipped an AI application, automations for a local startup, and unconventional growth systems—automated Reddit engagement, LinkedIn-scraped outreach, and end-to-end pipelines where I owned outcomes. This work became the center of my energy.

today

Today I’m active in hackathons, entrepreneurship clubs, and I intern at a VC firm. It sharpened my judgment: most ideas don’t matter; fewer execute. I want to be on the operating side building systems that create measurable outcomes.

My “why” is simple: build things that matter, with people who move fast and stay real.
